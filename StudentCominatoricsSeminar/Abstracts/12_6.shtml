The birth of k-schur functions

Anna Pun

Drexel University

Abstract

k-schur functions were introduced by Lapointe, Lascousx and Morse about 20 years ago in an attempt to give a combinatorial interpretation of the q, t Kostka coefficients in the schur expansion of (modified) Macdonald symmetric polynomials. This study led to a refinement of the Macdonald's positivity conjecture and furthermore generalize and refine the classical theory of Schur functions, Pieri rules, Youngâ€™s lattice, the Cauchy identity, tableaux, and Stanley symmetric functions. It also has strong connections in representation theory and algebraic geometry. In this talk, I will first review some basic definition and properties of symmetric functions, and then introduce Hall-Littlewood functions and Macdonald symmetric functions in more details and end by some examples and properties of k schur functions.
