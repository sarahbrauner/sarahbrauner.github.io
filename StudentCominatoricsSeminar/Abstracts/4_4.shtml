Enumerative properties of tournaments

Darij Grinberg

University of Minnesota

Abstract

Tournaments are directed simple graphs where each pair of distinct vertices is joined by an arc in exactly one direction. We discuss Redei's (insufficiently) famous theorem that any tournament has an odd number of Hamiltonian paths, and also the relation between tournaments and Vandermonde's determinant.
