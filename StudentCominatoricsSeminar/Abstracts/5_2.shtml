Student Combinatorics and Algebra Seminar
Thursday, May 2, 2019
4:40pm in Vincent 570



A totally non-negative tasting menu

Melissa Sherman-Bennett

UC Berkeley/Harvard University

Abstract

The totally nonnegative Grassmannian is the subset of the (real) Grassmannian where all Plucker coordinates are non-negative, introduced independently by Postnikov (2006) and Lusztig (1998). From a topological perspective, it's a very nice space; Postnikov, Speyer, and Williams (2009) showed that it is a CW complex, and Galashin, Karp, and Lam recently showed that it is a regular CW complex homeomorphic to a closed ball. It is also a very nice space from a combinatorial perspective, as the cells of the CW complex are in bijection with a variety of combinatorial objects, such as Le-diagrams, Grassmann necklaces, decorated permutations, and (equivalence classes of) reduced plabic graphs. I'll describe some of these objects and discuss what they tell us about the cells. Time permitting, I will also discuss how plabic graphs for the top dimensional cell feature in the cluster algebra structure on the coordinate ring of the complex Grassmannian.
