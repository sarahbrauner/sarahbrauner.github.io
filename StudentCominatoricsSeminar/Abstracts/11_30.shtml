(Fantastic) Tropical Varieties and Where to Find Them

Mahrud Sayrafi

University of Minnesota

Abstract

In tropical arithmetic, the sum of two numbers is their maximum and the product of two numbers is their usual sum. Interestingly, many results from algebra and geometry, including the Quadratic Formula, the Fundamental Theorem of Algebra, and Bezoutâ€™s Theorem, continue to hold in the tropical world. After a basic introduction, we will see how to draw tropical curves and, time permitting, I'll talk about how tropical geometry answers questions in topics ranging from biology and statistics to intersection theory.
