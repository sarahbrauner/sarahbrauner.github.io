An Entrance to Buildings

Andy Hardt

UMN

Abstract

Bruhat-Tits Buildings are simplicial complexes on which reductive groups act nicely. The group action illuminates the reductive group structure, and the main “pieces” of a reductive group–Borel subgroups, tori, parabolic subgroups, and the Weyl group–all play a role. The “apartments” of a finite building are Coxeter complexes, so the combinatorial structure is nice as well. Conversely, given a group action on a building, certain stabilizer subgroups must satisfy a set of axioms by Tits, and thereby form a “BN-pair”. Groups with a BN-pair have a lot of the same structure of reductive groups, so the concept of a building allows us to more easily find and study these groups. We will construct buildings from the ground up, talking about apartments first, and (hopefully!) giving illuminating examples along the way.
