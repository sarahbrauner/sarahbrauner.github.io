Reflection length in various settings

Joel Brewster Lewis

George Washington University

Abstract

Given a group G with a generating set T, it is natural to ask about the T-length of an element g of G, that is, the smallest number k such that g can be written as a product of k elements of T. In general, this quantity is uncomputable (in the rigorous CS/logic sense). However, when the group G and generating set T are nice, it may have nice interpretations. I'll survey some examples, in the context of linear and affine reflection groups over various fields, and give some idiosyncratic reasons to think this is interesting thing to think about.
