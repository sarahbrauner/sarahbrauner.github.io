Combinatorial Reciprocity Theorems

Sam Hopkins

University of Minnesota

Abstract

Often in combinatorics we have a sequence A_1, A_2, ... of sets of discrete objects (where we think of the elements of A_n as the objects of "size n"), such that #A_n is given by a polynomial P(n) in n. "Combinatorial reciprocity" is when the evaluation P(-n) of this polynomial at a negative integer is equal (up to a predictable sign) to #B_n, where B_1, B_2, ... is a related sequence of sets of combinatorial objects. I will survey the major examples of combinatorial reciprocity (binomials, order polynomials, chromatic functions, Ehrhart polynomials) and will hopefully explain the "master result" in this area: Stanley's reciprocity theorem.
