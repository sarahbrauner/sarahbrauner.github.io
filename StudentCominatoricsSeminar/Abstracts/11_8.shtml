Dessins d'enfants: Where Graph Theory meets Number Theory

Claire Frechette

University of Minnesota

Abstract

Grothendieck's "dessins d'enfants" are a kind of bicolored graph that can be equivalently described in combinatorially, topological, and number theoretic data. In particular, genus one dessins, (the ones which can be embedded on the torus) are equivalent to certain elliptic curves. We will explore the connections between these different characterizations, working out a nice example completely and some less nice examples partially.
