What is Cyclic Sieving, Anyway?

Eric Stucky

University of Minnesota

Abstract

The cyclic sieving phenomenon (CSP) was first described in 2004 by three UMN combinatorialists (Vic Reiner, Dennis Stanton, and Dennis White) as a generalization of Stembridge's "q=-1 phenomenon". It describes a rigorous sense in which a q-analogue can "know" about how a cyclic group acts on combinatorial objects. In this talk we use the motivating example of binomial coefficients to arrive at a definition of a CSP. From there we discuss a representation-theoretic framework to prove CSPs and, time permitting, use it to sketch a proof for our example.
